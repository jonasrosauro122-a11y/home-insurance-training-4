14-Day Training Plan

Tap or click a day to see the full lesson breakdown.

Day 1 — Getting to Know You & LAVA VA Roles

Different Roles of a LAVA VA, Admin Tasks

Topics:

  • Team introductions, company culture, overview of VA responsibilities, admin tasks

What to Expect:

  • Icebreaker activities and introductions to build team connection
  • Presentation about LAVA’s mission, values, and how VAs support insurance operations
  • Walkthrough of daily VA tasks — emails, data entry, scheduling, and client communication

What You’ll Learn:

  • The different VA roles (Admin, CSR, Data Entry, Rater Support, etc.)
  • Professional expectations: punctuality, communication, accountability
  • Overview of tools used (Google Workspace, Slack, CRM systems, etc.)

Goal:

Build familiarity with LAVA’s work culture and the foundation of VA responsibilities

Day 2 — Insurance 101: Policy Cycle

Basic insurance concepts, policy lifecycle, key terminology

Topics:

  • Basic insurance concepts, policy lifecycle, key terminology

What to Expect:

  • Interactive presentation on how insurance policies are created, issued, renewed, and cancelled
  • Real-world examples of client interactions during each policy stage

What You’ll Learn:

  • The flow from Quote → Bind → Issue → Endorsement → Renewal → Cancellation
  • Understanding common insurance terms (premium, deductible, coverage, limits, etc.)

Goal:

Gain a strong foundation in how insurance functions from start to finish

Day 3 — Insurance Documents

Forms, Declarations, and Document Types

Topics:

  • Forms, Declarations, and Document Types

What to Expect:

  • Guided breakdown of the most common insurance documents
  • Practice identifying information in sample policies

What You’ll Learn:

  • What a Declaration Page includes and how to read it
  • Common forms like ID cards, binders, COIs, and invoices
  • How to properly organize and label insurance files

Goal:

Build document literacy and confidence handling client files

Day 4 — English 101 & Mock Calls

Communication etiquette, CSR call handling, simulated calls

Topics:

  • Communication etiquette, CSR call handling, simulated calls

What to Expect:

  • Review of call and email etiquette for insurance clients
  • Mock call simulations to practice listening, empathy, and response clarity

What You’ll Learn:

  • Polite and professional English expressions for customer service
  • Handling difficult conversations and managing call flow
  • Pronunciation and tone exercises for clarity and confidence

Goal:

Improve communication skills for real client interactions

Day 5 — Intro to AMS & CRM

Basic navigation and data entry

Topics:

  • System navigation, data entry, client record management

What to Expect:

  • Hands-on practice logging into AMS and CRM platforms
  • Step-by-step guidance entering and updating client information

What You’ll Learn:

  • Key AMS modules (Clients, Policies, Certificates, Reports)
  • Basic CRM navigation — creating leads, updating status, managing notes
  • Importance of data accuracy and confidentiality

Goal:

Become comfortable navigating insurance systems and entering data properly

Day 6 — Raters & Carriers

Overview of rating systems and common carriers

Topics:

  • Introduction to rating systems, major insurance carriers

What to Expect:

  • Overview of how rating platforms calculate premiums
  • Demo of popular raters and explanation of carrier differences

What You’ll Learn:

  • The relationship between agencies, raters, and carriers
  • Common carrier portals (Safeco, Progressive, Travelers, etc.)
  • Basic troubleshooting tips for rating errors

Goal:

Understand how raters and carriers work together in quoting

Day 7 — Home Insurance Types

Coverages, construction knowledge & quoting

Topics:

  • Homeowners coverages, construction details, and quoting

What to Expect:

  • Discussion on HO3, HO4, HO6, and DP3 policy types
  • Real examples of home coverage breakdowns

What You’ll Learn:

  • Home insurance coverage parts (Dwelling, Liability, Personal Property, etc.)
  • How construction type and location affect premium
  • Basics of generating a home quote in a rater system

Goal:

Build foundational knowledge in property insurance

Day 8 — Auto Insurance Intro

Coverages, endorsements & quoting basics

Topics:

  • Auto coverages, endorsements, quoting basics

What to Expect:

  • Guided walkthrough of auto policy components
  • Practice exercises quoting different vehicle types

What You’ll Learn:

  • Coverages: Liability, Collision, Comprehensive, UM/UIM, etc.
  • Common auto endorsements and add-ons
  • Factors affecting premiums: driver history, vehicle, and location

Goal:

Develop quoting familiarity for auto insurance policies

Day 9 — Trailer / Boat / RV / Umbrella

Overview and quoting specifics

Topics:

  • Specialty insurance types and quoting differences

What to Expect:

  • Overview of coverage needs for non-standard vehicles and assets
  • Sample quotes using real carrier guidelines

What You’ll Learn:

  • How Trailer, Boat, RV, and Umbrella policies differ from auto/home
  • When to recommend or bundle these policies
  • Identifying cross-selling opportunities for clients

Goal:

Learn to assist in quoting and managing specialty insurance lines

Day 10 — Home Declaration Page Activity

Analyze sample home declaration & quote simulation

Topics:

  • Home Dec page analysis and quote simulation

What to Expect:

  • Group activity analyzing sample declaration pages
  • Simulated quoting task using provided client details

What You’ll Learn:

  • How to interpret coverage limits, deductibles, and effective dates
  • How to verify accuracy when entering information in AMS or CRM

Goal:

Apply knowledge to real-world document and quoting tasks

Day 11 — Auto Declaration Page Activity

Analyze sample auto declaration & quote simulation

Topics:

  • Auto Dec page analysis and quote simulation

What to Expect:

  • Group discussion identifying differences in multi-vehicle policies
  • Practice replicating quotes based on dec page details

What You’ll Learn:

  • Reading policy details such as VIN, coverage levels, and drivers
  • How to cross-check for missing or mismatched data

Goal:

Strengthen data entry accuracy and analytical skills

Day 12 — ACORD Forms

Personal and commercial ACORD files and key fields

Topics:

  • Personal and commercial ACORD forms, key fields, usage

What to Expect:

  • Deep dive into ACORD 25, 28, 80, and other common forms
  • Hands-on filling of ACORD samples

What You’ll Learn:

  • The purpose of each ACORD form
  • Required fields and how to avoid common errors
  • How to properly generate and send ACORDs through AMS

Goal:

Build confidence handling standard insurance documentation

Day 13 — SOP & Exam

SOP lecture, writing practice, and final exam

Topics:

  • Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs), review, and final assessment

What to Expect:

  • Review of all topics covered across training days
  • Writing activity: creating your own mini-SOP
  • Final exam (theory + practical exercises)

What You’ll Learn:

  • SOP structure and importance in consistency and compliance
  • How to document steps clearly for team reference
  • Self-assessment on readiness for production tasks

Goal:

Evaluate readiness and reinforce learned processes

Day 14 — Recognition & Certificates

Presentation of certificates and feedback

Topics:

  • Graduation, feedback, and recognition ceremony

What to Expect:

  • Review session and open discussion for feedback and reflections
  • Recognition of top performers and certificate distribution

What You’ll Learn:

  • Reflection on progress and confidence in handling VA tasks
  • Insights from peers and trainers
  • Motivation for continued learning and improvement

Goal:

Celebrate completion and transition from training to actual workflow
