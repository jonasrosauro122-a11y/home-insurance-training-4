Customer Success Leadership

The Customer Success Director (CSD) is responsible for providing strategic leadership, final decision authority, and cross-departmental alignment for Customer Success. This role establishes Customer Success standards, policies, and overall direction while serving as the primary escalation point for both client and internal matters. The CSD partners closely with Sales, IT, Finance, and Leadership to resolve complex issues, oversees client risk management and retention strategies, and approves final decisions that impact client accounts. For Level 2 changes, the Customer Success Director and the client hold final decision authority.

Account Management

The Account Manager serves as the primary client point of contact and supports the ongoing alignment, stability, and growth of assigned accounts. This role manages client relationships post-onboarding, conducts regular check-ins and account reviews, coordinates training requests and service support, identifies risks, gaps, or growth opportunities, and communicates client needs to internal teams as appropriate. Account Managers escalate matters based on impact, complexity, or policy considerations.

Executive Assistants

The Executive Assistant (EA) provides structured administrative and coordination support to the Customer Success Director (CSD) and Account Managers (AMs) to ensure clear communication, organized scheduling, and consistent documentation across client accounts. This role arranges and manages schedules for client and internal meetings, organizes and monitors email communications on behalf of assigned partners, attends client check-ins and onboarding meetings to support continuity and follow-up, creates and maintains documentation related to meetings, requests, and action items, and coordinates internal requests and follow-ups across departments based on direction from their working partner. The EA operates in a support and coordination capacity, ensuring information is documented, communicated, and routed appropriately while maintaining alignment with established processes and escalation paths.

Trainers

The Trainer’s primary objective is to deliver structured, client-requested training aligned with Lava standards and the approved training menus. This role conducts VA training sessions both onsite and online, delivers system, carrier, and insurance concept training, supports skill development and readiness, coordinates training delivery with Account Managers and Team Leads, and documents training completion and outcomes. All training is delivered in alignment with the approved Lava training menu, covering carriers, systems, AMS platforms, raters, insurance fundamentals, and ACORD forms.

Team Leaders

The Team Leader (TL) provides guidance, oversight, and consistency to the VAs assigned to their team and serves as the main point of contact for VA and Account Manager (AM) communications. This role conducts daily, weekly, and monthly check-in meetings with VAs, attends client check-in meetings with AMs, supports AMs with escalations and decision-making, reviews account health and performance trends, coordinates with Trainers to address skill gaps or training needs, ensures processes and standards are followed consistently, and escalates issues to Customer Success Leadership when appropriate. The TL serves as the first formal VA escalation point within Customer Success.
