Customer Success Team

Team expectations: The role of Customer Success (CS) is to ensure that clients achieve their desired outcomes while using Lava’s products or services, ultimately leading to retention, satisfaction, and growth.

It’s proactive, not reactive. Unlike customer support, which solves problems when they arise, Customer Success focuses on preventing issues and driving long-term value. Long-term value is achieved by aligning expectations and delivering results through training and coaching.

Team Roles

  • Customer Success Director: Primary department decision maker and accountable for department goals and objectives.
  • Account Managers: Primary point of contact for clients, provides ongoing support, proactively addresses concerns.
  • Trainers: Conduct classes and create materials to support learning for new hires and existing VAs.
  • Team Leads: Handle VA queries and coaching, coordinate with departments, monitor VA performance, and help improve task handling.

Onboarding

During onboarding, we prioritize alignment. We walk clients through our strategic plan for the year so there is clear visibility into VA development, support, and growth.

Expectations are clearly defined for both sides: training support, performance monitoring, accountability, optimization guidance from us; and clear direction, timely feedback, system access, and defined priorities from the client.

Ground rules on communication, performance standards, and collaboration are set from day one. VAs are reminded of three critical tasks they must never do: solicit insurance advice, bind a policy, and do premium-bearing changes.

We review required materials, workflows, and system access to ensure VAs are fully equipped for success. This structure prevents misalignment and sets the foundation for long-term growth.

Endorsements

  • Identify minor issues and endorse to the appropriate departments for proactive management.
  • New tasks or retraining required: Training from Insurance or Automations Training Department, Build not being used, PM meetings, VA behavioral or attendance issues, TL coaching, AM discussion with client, IR escalation to NTE from HR.
  • Issues within the Agency: AM meeting with client, VA intends to quit, TL coaching, endorsement to AM for possible client meeting, VA performance issues handled via coaching, PIP, or IR escalation.
